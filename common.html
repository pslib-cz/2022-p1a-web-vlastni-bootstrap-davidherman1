The Cascades frog

The Cascades frog, also known as the Cascades Mountain frog, is a small species of frog found in the Pacific Northwest region of North America, particularly in the Cascade Range of Washington and Oregon. They are typically brown or green in color, with dark spots on their back, and have a distinctive dark stripe that runs through their eye and down their snout. These frogs are semi-aquatic, spending much of their time in or near shallow streams and ponds, where they feed on insects and other small invertebrates. Unfortunately, like many amphibian species, Cascades frogs are facing threats from habitat loss, climate change, and disease, making conservation efforts all the more important.

The Tree Frog

tree frog, a small arboreal frog found throughout much of Europe and parts of Asia. They are typically bright green in color with a white or yellow stripe running down their sides and a dark stripe through their eye. These frogs are nocturnal and spend much of their time in trees, shrubs, and other vegetation near bodies of water. They have a distinctive high-pitched call that can be heard during the breeding season, which typically occurs from May to August. Despite being common in some areas, European tree frogs face threats from habitat loss and fragmentation, pollution, and climate change. Conservation efforts, such as preserving and restoring wetland habitats, are essential for their survival.

the Spotted litter frog

Philictimantis maculatus, also known as the Spotted litter frog, is a small species of frog found in the forests of West and Central Africa. They are typically brown or gray in color with a series of dark spots and stripes on their back and legs. These frogs are primarily terrestrial, spending much of their time on the forest floor where they feed on small invertebrates. They are known for their unique reproductive behavior, which involves the female laying her eggs on the forest floor and the male guarding them until they hatch. Unfortunately, like many amphibian species, Spotted litter frogs are facing threats from habitat loss, climate change, and disease, making conservation efforts all the more important.

The Leopard frog

Leopard frog, is a species of frog found in North America, from southern Canada to northern Mexico. They are named for their striking leopard-like spots and can range in color from green to brown. These frogs are semi-aquatic, spending much of their time in or near bodies of water, where they feed on insects, small fish, and other prey. Leopard frogs are also known for their ability to jump great distances, using their powerful hind legs to propel themselves up to 3 feet in a single leap. While once common, Leopard frogs are facing threats from habitat loss, pollution, and climate change. Conservation efforts, such as restoring and protecting wetlands and other critical habitats, are important for their survival.
